Finley Robotics Scholarship

Each year the Finley Robotics Initiative awards up to ten Reachy Mini robots to exceptional high school students who lack access to robotics programs. In addition to receiving a robot, scholars join a community of mentors and peers and participate in project challenges designed to build critical thinking and technical skills.

Eligibility

  • Students entering grades 9 through 11 at the time of application.
  • Demonstrated financial need or enrollment in a school without a robotics program.
  • A strong interest in robotics, artificial intelligence or creative technology.
  • Curiosity, initiative and perseverance—qualities that do not always show up on a transcript.

Application Requirements

  • Personal statement: In 500 words or less, tell us why robotics excites you and how you plan to use the Reachy Mini to explore new ideas.
  • Recommendation: One short reference letter from a teacher, counselor or mentor who can speak to your curiosity and work ethic.
  • Optional video: Submit a brief video (1–2 minutes) introducing yourself and describing a problem you would like to solve with technology.
  • Proof of need: A note from a school official or other documentation indicating a lack of access to robotics resources.

Selection Process

Applications are reviewed holistically. We value passion for learning and a willingness to experiment more than grades or test scores. A panel of educators and engineers evaluates each submission based on:

  • Enthusiasm for robotics and AI.
  • Evidence of initiative and self‑motivation.
  • Ability to articulate goals and potential projects.
  • Need for access to robotics tools.

Timeline

The 2026 application cycle opens on September 1 and closes on November 30. Finalists will be notified by January 15, and recipients will receive their Reachy Mini robots in early February. Scholars participate in monthly virtual check‑ins throughout the spring to share progress and receive guidance.

Expectations of Scholars

Recipients agree to:

  • Participate in at least one virtual mentorship session per month.
  • Document their projects and share updates on our showcase page.
  • Treat the equipment with care and use it for educational purposes.

We encourage scholars to explore, fail, iterate and ask questions. There is no single path—your curiosity will guide the journey.

If you are ready to embark on this adventure, please contact us using the form on the Contact page. We look forward to hearing your story.
