Meet Reachy Mini

Reachy Mini is an expressive companion robot designed for human‑robot interaction, creative coding and AI exploration. Developed by Pollen Robotics and now maintained by Hugging Face, Reachy Mini combines approachable hardware with a fully open‑source software stack. Its compact design and friendly personality make it ideal for students and hobbyists.

Key Features

  • Open‑source platform: All hardware designs and software libraries are openly available, encouraging modification and community contributions.
  • Expressive motion: Nine degrees of freedom enable head rotations, translations and antenna movements for lifelike gestures.
  • Sensory suite: A high‑resolution camera, four digital microphones and a 5 W speaker allow vision, sound and speech interaction.
  • On‑board compute: Powered by a Raspberry Pi Compute Module 4 with Wi‑Fi and Bluetooth for standalone operation or tethered control.
  • Battery powered: The included LiFePO4 battery provides portable use with built‑in protection circuitry.
  • Python SDK: A simple Python library exposes control of motors, sensors and behaviors, making it accessible to beginners.

Specifications at a Glance

  • Dimensions: 30 cm tall, 20 cm long and 15.5 cm wide; weighs about 1.5 kg.
  • Degrees of Freedom: 9 DoF (6 in the head, 1 in the body and 2 in the antennae).
  • Sensors: Raspberry Pi v3 wide‑angle camera and four PDM MEMS microphones.
  • Actuators: Dynamixel motors controlling the base, antennae and Stewart platform.
  • Power: 6.8 – 7.6 V LiFePO4 battery (2000 mAh).
  • Price: About $449 (subject to change).

For more technical details, visit the official Reachy Mini page on Hugging Face. The Finley Robotics Initiative uses Reachy Mini because its open design encourages learning and experimentation.
